Mailbox Peak

08/24/2020

The fact that anyone still hikes the god-forsaken thing that is the old Mailbox Peak trail is absolutely baffling to me. Let me give you some numbers. The nearby Mt. Si trail is a reasonably strenuous training hike. Over a four-mile route, that trail ascends some 3,150 feet. For those keeping score, that’s an average grade of 18.5%, or a little less than 1 foot of elevation gain for every 5 feet traversed. The old Mailbox Peak trail packs a heavier punch at 4,000 feet of ascension. But, here’s the kicker, it does so in only 2.7 miles. Grade: 28.0%.

To my knowledge, this is the only day-hike on the I-90 corridor that was rebuilt not because of storm damage or forest management, but because people kept getting hurt on the darn thing. Today, the far more reasonable route tackles the elevation gain over 10 miles. However, the old path occasionally cuts across the present trail as a stark reminder of Mailbox’s gluttony for punishment.

Despite its hobbling, Mailbox packs a punch worthy of the early drive to secure a parking spot. There are only a few flat sections to give brief respite from the constant plodding uphill. Don’t reserve water breaks for “the next switchback”; they can be spaced out as far as a mile. And, don’t mistake a break in the trees for victory. Upon rising out of the tree line to a field of talus, you’ll be met with arguably the most difficult section that climbs 700 feet over a half mile. If you took the new trail, this is what you were missing from the old.

While trying to ignore the lactic acid in your legs, take a moment to recognize the fantastic trail work that went into building stairs through the rocky section out of individual boulders. We’re lucky to have the WTA and its volunteers; my ankles would like to personally thank them.

Following the rocks and a short scamper up a thinly-vegetated stretch, we come to the pièce de résistance: the mailboxes on top of Mailbox. Yes, you read right, there are now two of the things up there. In addition to the true mailbox, with its commemorative plaque and fine crust of graffiti, is a wimpy appendix on the eastern side of the summit. Don’t miss the DNR datum from 1983 a few steps away from the main attraction. Many have written about the wide array of items one can find stashed in the mailbox. Some highlights from my visit include:

  • Biden campaign mail
  • A full bottle of Fireball whiskey
  • A cool pen
  • Mysterious Amazon Prime packages

The Fantasy Costco vibe wears off pretty quickly when you realize that most of the stuff people leave in the mailbox is pretty much a step or two above trash. The WTA claims that there is a summit registry, but I didn’t see the wisdom in digging around a cursed public receptacle during a pandemic for the sake of bravado.

On the way back down, I couldn’t help but feel a little underwhelmed by the reality of the legend. Perhaps the new trail takes away from Mailbox’s identity, demoting it to 1.5x Mt. Si a few exits past North Bend. Can one really call Mailbox an adventure if there’s a bus stop and a ready-made rock staircase just below its summit? At the very least, more can share in the joy of some of the finest views in the Snoqualmie valley. And, maybe the legend truly lives in the hearts of the hikers who keep coming back for more.
