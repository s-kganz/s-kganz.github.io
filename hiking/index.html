Hiking Log

Lake Serene

6/2/2019 Lake Serene is the Mt. Si of the Highway 2 corridor. That is to say - strenuous yet beautiful hiking and lots of people. My first experience with the trail was a line of some 20+ signs saying “NO PARKING” on the side of the road. After parking in what was probably the last spot in the lot (at like 7 am!!) and starting up the trail, I was soon walking behind a group playing music out of a speaker.

Mt. Si

12/31/2019 (most recently) One cannot recreate in Washington without eventually going to Mt. Si. The trail is well-maintained, connects to other long-distance trails, and you can literally take a bus to it from Seattle. I haven’t personally taken that route, but dang would it be cool to get to a hike via Orca card. In fact, taking the bus is probably the optimal way to get to Mt. Si. The hike’s extreme popularity means that the parking lot, already quite large, fills up in the morning during the peak season.

Mt. Washington

7/5/2020 Fun story about this one. With the pandemic still in full swing, masks are an essential part of any form of outdoor recreation. So, you can imagine how silly I felt when after 10 minutes of driving to Mt. Washington, I realized that I had left my mask at home. Or, so I thought. After making the drive back and opening my trunk, there the mask was. I felt a little sullen over wasting a half hour in transit.
