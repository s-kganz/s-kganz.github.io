Lake Serene

6/2/2019

Lake Serene is the Mt. Si of the Highway 2 corridor. That is to say - strenuous yet beautiful hiking and lots of people. My first experience with the trail was a line of some 20+ signs saying “NO PARKING” on the side of the road. After parking in what was probably the last spot in the lot (at like 7 am!!) and starting up the trail, I was soon walking behind a group playing music out of a speaker.

So yeah… good start.

If people are a problem for you, save this one for the off season and bring spikes. But if you can deal with a conga line, Lake Serene is probably the most spectacular hike on Highway 2. Nearby Bridal Veil Falls feeds the lower sections of the trail with quaint ponds and creeks. Absolutely no issues with the trail, probably owing to the maintenance warranted by the crowds.

Roughly half way up the trail you come to a turn off for Bridal Veil Falls. Take this on your way up; you will be rewarded with a torrential waterfall paired with your first good view of the ridgelines on the north side of the highway.

The trail becomes a little more difficult as you get closer to the lake. But press on and reach the crown jewel of this trail: over a square mile of open water framed by scree and snowmelt.

No overnight use is permitted, but as you get closer to the lake there are plenty of small clearings to use as lunch spots. The water is pure snowmelt and a welcome top-off for what you drank on the way up. As an added bonus, the difference in elevation from the lake to the ridge top on the south side of the lake is roughly the same as the elevation you just climbed on the trail!

Swimmers are a common site, but do take care to practice leave no trace. When I visited, there were plenty of people being disrespectful of other users (see: playing music, dropping beer cans, etc.). One would hope that the lake will remain in pristine conditions for future generations of hikers.

In short: maybe save this one for the fall. And, bring a trash bag to clean up what others may have left behind.
