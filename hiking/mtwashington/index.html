Mt. Washington

7/5/2020

Fun story about this one. With the pandemic still in full swing, masks are an essential part of any form of outdoor recreation. So, you can imagine how silly I felt when after 10 minutes of driving to Mt. Washington, I realized that I had left my mask at home. Or, so I thought. After making the drive back and opening my trunk, there the mask was.

I felt a little sullen over wasting a half hour in transit. Today, after all, was fourth of July weekend and I had made the somewhat foolhardy decision of picking a popular hike. These feelings were perhaps compounded by the fact that I then got lost in the entrance to Olallie state park while looking for the Mt. Washington trailhead.

To anyone else planning on hiking Mt. Washington, here’s the way to the trailhead in as simple of instructions as I can provide. On I-90 going east, take exit 38. and go right at the T-intersection. Then, take the next right immediately. The WTA page says you will cross a river before finding the turnoff. This is true, but you can’t really see the river from the road (or, maybe I’m just blind). Continue down this dirt road to the Homestead Valley Trailhead. Despite the name difference, this is the right spot.

Start on the trail, going rapidly uphill. You will come to the wide, graded, gravel-paved Palouse to Cascades trail (fun fact: this trail continually traverses almost the entire state of Washington!). Go right (west) and look for an intersection with a narrow footpath on the left (south) side. This trail should be marked for Mt. Washington.

Now, confident that you are in fact not lost, begin your walk upward. As you traverse the gradual uphill grade, look out for cool rock faces on the upslope side of the trail. Climbing is popular in the area, with pitons marking out the most common routes.

The trail continues upward before opening up somewhat at a ridge saddle. The trail makes a sharp bend coincident with a different trail, but the route then turns straight up the final ridge to the peak of Mt. Washington. As you near the summit, be sure to check behind you for good views of Diablo Lake. The summit itself is somewhat small, so consider taking lunch somewhat earlier.

When I reached the summit (thankfully I was a few minutes ahead of the crowds), the morning clouds had not yet burned off. But just a few moments before I got up to leave, they parted enough for me to get this picture!

Maybe it was a good thing I thought I forgot my mask :)

Have fun, and stay safe out there!
